# YKI Personalized Learning Material — Learner Questionnaire

This questionnaire collects background information to generate personalized YKI exam preparation materials. All information is used only for content personalization — nothing private or sensitive is needed.

Answer in any language you're comfortable with. Skip any question that doesn't apply.

---

## 1. Languages & Communication

1. What is your native language (mother tongue)?
2. What other languages do you speak? How well? (basic / conversational / fluent)
3. How long have you been studying Finnish?
4. How would you describe your current Finnish level? (beginner / intermediate / advanced)
5. Which Finnish skills are strongest? (reading / writing / listening / speaking)
6. Which skills need the most work?
7. Do you use Finnish daily? In what situations? (work, shopping, friends, officials...)
8. Do you watch Finnish TV/media or listen to Finnish radio/podcasts?

## 2. Origin & Cultural Background

9. Where are you originally from? (country / region)
10. Where else have you lived before Finland? For how long?
11. What cultural traditions or holidays are important to you and your family?
12. What food from your home culture do you make or miss the most?
13. Is there anything about your cultural background that often comes up in conversations with Finns?

## 3. Life in Finland

14. How long have you lived in Finland?
15. Where in Finland do you live? (city / neighborhood)
16. What type of housing do you live in? (apartment, house, rental, owned)
17. What is your immigration status? (residence permit, PR, citizen, applying...)
18. How did you end up in Finland? (work, family, study, other)
19. What has been the best thing about living in Finland?
20. What has been the most challenging thing?

## 4. Family & Social Life

21. Do you have a spouse/partner? What is their background?
22. Do you have children? Ages?
23. Are your children in Finnish daycare/school? How is it going?
24. Do you have family members in Finland? In your home country?
25. How do you stay in touch with family abroad?
26. Do you have Finnish friends? How did you meet them?
27. What communities or groups are you part of in Finland?

## 5. Work & Education

28. What is your educational background? (degree, field)
29. What work did you do before Finland?
30. What work do you do now in Finland? (or looking for work / studying / at home)
31. If looking for work — what field? What challenges have you faced?
32. Have you taken any courses in Finland? (Finnish language, professional, other)
33. Have you dealt with Finnish authorities for work? (TE-toimisto, Kela, verotoimisto)

## 6. Daily Life & Interests

34. What is a typical weekday like for you?
35. What hobbies or activities do you enjoy?
36. How do you get around? (car, bus, metro, bike, walk)
37. Where do you usually shop for groceries?
38. Do you exercise regularly? What kind?
39. What do you do to relax or deal with stress?

## 7. Finnish Experiences & Opinions

40. What Finnish customs or habits have surprised you?
41. Have you experienced Finnish sauna culture? What do you think?
42. What Finnish food do you like? What don't you like?
43. Have you been to a Finnish mökki (cottage)? How was it?
44. What do you think about the Finnish healthcare system? (from your experience)
45. What do you think about Finnish bureaucracy? (Kela, Migri, DVV, etc.)
46. What do you think about Finnish weather/seasons?
47. Have you experienced any cultural misunderstandings in Finland?

## 8. Goals & Future

48. Why are you taking the YKI test? (citizenship, work, personal goal)
49. Have you taken YKI before? How did it go?
50. What areas of the test worry you the most? (puhuminen, kirjoittaminen, kuunteleminen, lukeminen)
51. Where do you see yourself in 5 years?
52. What would you like to achieve in Finland?
53. Is there anything specific you want to be able to say or write in Finnish?

---

**Notes for the content generator:**
- Use answers to create relatable scenarios, not literal autobiography
- The learner's background informs themes, not exact facts
- Content should be applicable to the learner's real daily situations
- Cultural references should feel natural, not forced
- All Finnish at YKI keskitaso level with natural spoken style (mä/mun/sun)
