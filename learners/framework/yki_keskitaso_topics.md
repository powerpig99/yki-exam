# YKI Keskitaso (Levels 3-4 / B1-B2) — Comprehensive Topic & Test Reference

Last updated: 2026-02-17

---

## 1. Test Structure

### Overview
| Section | Finnish Name | Duration | Format |
|---------|-------------|----------|--------|
| Reading | Tekstin ymmärtäminen / Lukeminen | ~60 min | Paper, pencil |
| Writing | Kirjoittaminen | 55 min | Paper, pencil |
| Listening | Puheen ymmärtäminen / Kuunteleminen | ~45 min | Language studio, headphones |
| Speaking | Puhuminen | ~30 min | Language studio, recorded |

Total test time: ~3-5 hours including breaks.

### Scoring
- Each section scored independently: levels 1-6 (mapping to CEFR A1-C2)
- Keskitaso targets levels 3 (B1) and 4 (B2)
- For Finnish citizenship: minimum taitotaso 3 (B1.1) in all sections
- You CAN get different levels in different sections

## 2. Speaking Test (Puhuminen) — Detailed Structure

### Task Flow
1. **Lämmittely** (warm-up) — vocabulary exercises
2. **Dialogit** (dialogues) — phone conversations, service situations
3. **Tilannetehtävät** (situation tasks) — quick reactions, 20-25 seconds each
4. **Kertominen** (narration) — 1-2 minute monologue on a personal topic
5. **Mielipide** (opinion) — 2 minute monologue, choose from 2 topics

### Task Details

**Dialogues (Phone conversations)**
- Simulated phone calls: you hear prompts, respond naturally
- Examples from actual tests:
  - Reporting stolen bike to police
  - Resolving unpaid phone bill
  - Calling about item for sale
  - Calling train station about family trip schedules
  - Renting a party venue

**Situation Tasks (Quick reactions, ~20-25 sec each)**
- 5 situations, hear audio prompt, respond when "Puhu nyt" plays
- Examples from actual tests:
  - Asking neighbor for help moving furniture
  - Telling department store about lost item
  - Telling friend you'll be late to birthday party
  - Explaining health issue at pharmacy
  - Apologizing for delay

**Kertominen (Narration, 1-2 min)**
- 1 minute to think, 1.5-2 minutes to speak
- Examples from actual tests:
  - Describe someone important to you and why
  - Talk about gender roles in household work
  - Describe visiting patterns and hospitality

**Mielipide (Opinion, 2 min)**
- Choose from 2 topics, justify your opinion
- Examples from actual tests:
  - Family finances (where money goes, who decides, joint/separate accounts, children's allowance)
  - Medication advertising (do people use too many medicines, is advertising good/bad)
  - Own apartment vs renting
  - Smartphones — people use them too much

## 3. Writing Test (Kirjoittaminen) — Detailed Structure

### Duration: 55 minutes, 3 tasks

**Task 1: Informal/Short Message**
- Viesti, postikortti, tekstiviesti
- Short, personal, to friend/family
- Examples: message about unexpected work trip, postcard from holiday

**Task 2: Formal Letter/Email**
- Valitus, kirje, virallinen viesti
- To authority, company, organization
- Examples: letter to organization requesting volunteer work, email to housing committee chairperson, complaint to service provider

**Task 3: Opinion Piece (Mielipide)**
- Choose from 2 topics
- Write for internet forum, newspaper, or similar
- Examples: "Own apartment vs renting", "Summer cottage — best place in the world", "People use smartphones too much"

### Key Assessment Criteria
- Clarity of content and understandability
- Text structure and coherence
- Register appropriateness (formal vs informal)
- Grammar and vocabulary (not required to be perfect)
- Ability to write about abstract topics (not just concrete/personal)
- **Write MORE rather than less** — assessors need enough text to evaluate

## 4. Listening Test (Puheen ymmärtäminen)

- ~20 questions: true/false, multiple choice (A/B/C), short written answers
- All audio played TWICE at keskitaso
- Content: conversations, announcements, news, natural speech
- Speakers use natural pace with some slang/colloquial Finnish
- Reported as challenging — practice with real Finnish media essential

---

## 5. Reading Test (Tekstin ymmärtäminen)

- ~24 questions: true/false, multiple choice, written responses
- ~6 texts, mostly under one page each (400-600 words at B1-B2)
- Content types: news articles, advertisements, instructions, notices
- Examples from actual tests: escaped lizard news story, bicycle repair shop ad, preventing frozen pipes guide
- Typically enough time to complete

## 6. Official YKI Topic Themes (7 Themes)

Based on Opetushallitus framework, Gimara.fi courses, and textbook structure:

### Theme 1: Ihminen ja lähipiiri (Person & Inner Circle)
- Family members and relationships
- Describing people (appearance, personality)
- Neighbors and neighborhood relations
- Household chores and responsibilities
- Different family types, single parenthood
- Friendship and social relationships
- Finnish celebrations: itsenäisyyspäivä, joulu, isänpäivä, juhannus, vappu
- Life events (weddings, births, funerals)

### Theme 2: Arkielämä ja asiointi (Daily Life & Services)
- Shopping (groceries, clothes, furniture)
- Banking and financial services
- Post office and mail
- Scheduling and time management
- Car-related matters (driving, repair, inspection)
- Directions and navigation
- Housing (renting, buying, taloyhtiö)
- Moving, apartments, furnishing
- Coffee/tea culture, restaurants
- Phone and internet services

### Theme 3: Yhteiskunta (Society)
- Finnish identity and culture (suomalaisuus)
- Government services: Kela, Migri, DVV, TE-toimisto, verotoimisto
- Elections and political participation
- Finnish history and geography
- Police and legal matters
- Insurance
- Vehicle inspection (katsastus)
- Immigration and integration
- Media and news

### Theme 4: Luonto ja ympäristö (Nature & Environment)
- Seasons and weather
- Finnish nature (forests, lakes, animals)
- Nature conservation
- Recycling and waste sorting (jätteiden lajittelu)
- Everyman's rights (jokamiehenoikeudet)
- Berry picking, mushroom foraging
- Outdoor activities (hiking, camping)
- Natural disasters
- Climate change and environmental issues

### Theme 5: Vapaa-aika ja harrastukset (Free Time & Hobbies)
- Sports and exercise
- Cultural activities (theater, music, art)
- Volunteer work
- Vacation and travel
- Finnish cottage culture (mökki)
- Suggestions and making plans
- Visiting etiquette
- Media consumption (TV, reading, internet)

### Theme 6: Terveys ja hyvinvointi (Health & Well-being)
- Doctor's visits, symptoms, illnesses
- Pharmacy and medication
- Emergency situations and first aid
- Dentist visits
- Occupational health
- Mental health and well-being
- Sleep and rest
- Anatomy and body
- Pregnancy and parenting
- Aging
- Diets and nutrition
- Substances and alcohol
- Accidents

### Theme 7: Työ ja koulutus (Work & Education)
- Professions and careers
- Job seeking and applications
- Job interviews
- Employment contracts
- Workplace culture and meetings
- Phone conversations at work
- Education system (school, university, vocational)
- Adult education and lifelong learning
- Entrepreneurship
- Remote work and work-life balance

## 8. Key Preparation Strategies

### Speaking
- Practice thinking in Finnish, not translating
- For kertominen: tell stories fluently, don't stop mid-sentence
- For mielipide: structure as "mun mielestä... toisaalta... mutta..."
- Speak MORE rather than less — silence is worse than imperfect speech
- Practice phone conversation format (can't see the other person)

### Writing
- Write as much as possible — too much is better than too little
- Know the difference between informal (mä, mun, sun) and formal (minä, hyvä virkailija)
- Practice: viesti (informal), valitus/kirje (formal), mielipide (opinion)
- Structure opinion pieces: position → argument → example → conclusion
- Don't aim for perfection — clarity beats grammar

### Listening
- Listen to YLE news, podcasts, Finnish radio
- Practice with natural speed Finnish (not textbook audio)
- Focus on main ideas, not every word
- YLE YKItreenit provides good practice material

### Reading
- Read Finnish news articles, ads, instructions
- Practice scanning for specific information
- Build vocabulary around all 7 themes

## 10. Gap Analysis vs Our Existing Materials

### Well Covered (Sections 01-08)
All 7 official themes are thoroughly covered across our 112 existing videos. Section 08 specifically adds immigration, digital life, banking, authorities, transport, culture, everyday problems, and health topics.

### Potential Gaps to Address for New Learners
- **Phone conversation format** — our dialogues are A/B but actual test uses studio recording with audio prompts
- **Timed responses** — test has strict time limits (20 sec for situations, 1.5-2 min for narration)
- **Reading comprehension topics** — our materials focus on speaking/writing; reading practice with ads, news, instructions could be added
- **Listening practice** — separate from our karaoke format; natural-speed audio needed
- **Formal register practice** — more valitus/kirje practice for kirjoittaminen
- **Abstract opinion topics** — topics beyond personal experience (society, politics, environment)

### New Topics Discovered from Research
- Medication advertising (appeared in actual test)
- Gender roles in housework (appeared in actual test)
- Frozen pipes prevention (appeared in reading)
- Pet/animal care scenarios
- Workplace phone conversations specifically
- Consumer rights disputes
